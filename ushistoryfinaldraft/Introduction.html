Introduction

Background and Argument

History is often substantially impacted by the ideals of the philosophical movements in that era. What made the 17th century scientists so prolific was not that they are more intelligent compared to the medieval scientists, but that they were born in an era that promotes science and logic. Similarly, revolutions in the Age of Enlightenment were not merely caused by the tyrannical governments. While what directly contributed to the American Revolution was Britain’s disrespect of the colonists’ rights and the inappropriately distributed power of the Parliament, the root of the revolution was the rebellious ideologies originated in various Enlightenment thinkers and spread in the colonies in 1760s and 1770s.
