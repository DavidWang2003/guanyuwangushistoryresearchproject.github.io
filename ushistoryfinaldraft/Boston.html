Boston Tea Party

Although the American Revolution was mostly caused by Enlightenment ideologies, it should be noted that material interest is also one of the drives of the revolution. In fact, materialist interest was the primary cause of the Boston Tea Party, a major protest against British policy two years before the revolution. (Carp 2012, 358-359) At the time, tea smuggling was prevalent and Sons of Liberty aligned interest with the smugglers. Therefore, the British parliament purposefully lowered the price of the tea so that their tea could be sold, which reduced the amount of smuggling and angered radicals like Sons of Liberty. (Farrand 1898, 268) Driven by profits, the radicals dumped 342 chests of tea in Boston, which added to the tension between Britain and the colonists and induced the revolution.
