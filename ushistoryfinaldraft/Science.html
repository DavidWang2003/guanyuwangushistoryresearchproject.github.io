Science

The pursuit of science, which is brought by the Enlightenment, was one of the driver of the Revolution. (Hindle 1976, 223) The “harmonious, orderly nature” that the colonists envisioned was inspired by “The popularizers of Newton's cosmology”. (Lokken 1996, 85) Because of science, the colonists wanted a government that corresponds to the harmonious nature. As the parliament violated the social contract, the colonists believed that the harmonious nature between the rulers and the ruled has been broken, so they decided to rebel against such government.
